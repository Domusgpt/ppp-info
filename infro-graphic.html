Traditional models process data sequentially, breaking down complex, interconnected problems into linear steps. This approach struggles with the sheer volume and high dimensionality of modern data from sources like robotics, finance, and AI, leading to bottlenecks and a loss of holistic context.

The PPP Solution

PPP represents a system's entire state as a single 4D geometric object (a polytope). Computation is redefined as a single, holistic transformation of this object. This embraces complexity, preserving the rich relationships within the data and enabling massively parallel processing.

Core Mechanics

PPP is built on three foundational principles that transform how machines process information.

Data as Geometry

System states are encoded into 4D polytopes. All variables and their relationships are captured in the vertices, edges, and faces of a single geometric structure.

üîÑ

Computation as Rotation

Instead of logical operations, computation is a rotation of the 4D polytope through its 6 rotational planes. Input data perturbs the shape, and its new orientation is the result.

ü§ñ

Output for Machines

The result is a 3D "shadow projection" of the 4D object. This visual output is a dense data format optimized for interpretation by modern AI and computer vision models.

A Leap in Rotational Freedom

The move from 3D to 4D space provides a qualitative leap in computational capability. 4D rotation operates on 6 independent planes, double the 3 axes available in 3D space, allowing for far more complex transformations in a single operation.

The Robotics Breakthrough: Direct IMU Mapping

The 6 rotational planes of 4D space map directly to the 6-Degrees-of-Freedom (6-DoF) motion captured by a standard Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU). This enables a robot's physical movement to be the computation itself, providing a revolutionary foundation for GPS-denied navigation.
